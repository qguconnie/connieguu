About Myself

I love art, technology & sports!

I am a junior at Wellesley College pursuing a double major in Media Arts and Sciences (MAS) and Psychology. My passion lies at the intersection of art and technology, with a focus on UI/UX design, graphic design, architecture, and game design. As a student researcher at the Wellesley HCI lab and Youth Media and Wellbeing lab, I leverage my interdisciplinary knowledge to study the impact of digital technology on adolescent development.

When not occupied with academics or research, I enjoy participating in sports such as soccer and frisbee. My leisure activities include playing video games, board games, and building with Legos with friends.

Helping Middle School Girls Create Their Digital Spaces

In summer 2021, I facilitated a virtual workshop for underrepresented middle school girls. I lead the participants through STEM identity exploration and UI/UX design related activities. Post workshop, I co-authored a paper under the topic of Innovating Novel Online Social Spaces with Diverse Middle School Girls. The paper was published on ACM Digital Library and I presented my research at CHI 2022.
