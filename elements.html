Theater Solo Performance: The Hell Screen

This performance centers on a narcissistic and psychopathic painter, Yoshihide, who insists to inflict tortures upon his apprentices in order to paint. The story climaxes when Yoshihide asks the lord to burn a lady in a carriage so he can finish a painting, not knowing his own daughter is going to be burnt. Written by Akutagaw, the story aims to highlight the conflict between art and moral. Performance style is inspired by theater artist Steven Berkoff. Acrobatic physical movements and minimalism is used for the audience to focus on the physicality of the performer.
*This is part of my Theater assessment in May 2020 IB exam.
